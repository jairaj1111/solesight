SoleSight — logo options

Five directions, each shown on paper and on ink. All vector — any can become favicon, nav mark, social avatar.

A · The Scopemark + wordmark

A lens/target — "sight" as instrument. The acid arc suggests a gauge filling. Evolves your current ◎ nav mark; the standalone circle works at 16px favicon size.

B · The Highlightpure wordmark

The site's signature move — the skewed acid highlighter stroke under an italic serif — promoted to identity. Zero icons; pure editorial confidence. Weakness: needs the full word, no tiny mark. → See the refined B family (B1–B4)

C · The Eyeicon + caps

Literal "sight": an open eye with an acid pupil watching the market. Letter-spaced caps read techy/institutional — closest to a data-company identity.

D · The Tickicon + wordmark

One continuous line: a sneaker's sole profile that breaks upward into a rising price arrow, over an acid baseline. The most "market for sneakers" concept — the whole pitch in one stroke.

E · The Chipapp-icon monogram

A serif italic S beside two rising bars, boxed like an app icon. Best for avatars, favicons, social — instantly at home on a phone screen. Pairs with any of the wordmarks above.

F · The Treadicon + wordmark

A shoe sole seen from below — but the tread blocks are a bar chart narrowing toward an acid peak. "Sole" and "data" in one shape; strongest pun of the set, still clean at small sizes.

G · The Pulseicon + wordmark

A shoelace that beats like a market pulse, ending in an acid aglet. "The pulse of the sneaker market" — energetic, reads equally as heartbeat and price chart.

H · The Stampbadge / seal

An authentication seal — the visual language of legit-checks and verified tags, which sneakerheads trust instinctively. Great as a hero watermark, sticker, or report stamp; heavier than the others as a everyday nav mark.
