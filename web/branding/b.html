The B family — refined

Four elevations of the Highlight direction. Big on paper, then small on ink and on acid.

B1 · The Swashtapered underline ribbon

The rectangle becomes a hand-pulled ribbon — thick where the pen lands under the S, tapering as it sweeps beneath Sight with a 1.3° tilt. Calligraphic, alive, still minimal.

B2 · The Markerhighlighter behind the word

The site's hero move exactly: one wobbled highlighter pass sitting behind the italic word, ink printed over it. Nothing parallel, nothing square — that's what makes it read hand-made rather than CSS. Brand rule: on dark surfaces this lockup always rides a paper chip.

B3 · The Periodultra-minimal

Everything italic, one acid full-stop set right where the sentence ends. The quiet-luxury read — the whole brand hangs on a single dot, and the dot alone becomes the favicon.

B4 · The Tittlethe secret detail

The dot of the i — the tittle — replaced by an acid eye quietly watching the market. No underline, no highlight; the entire brand accent is eight pixels wide. The detail people notice the third time and never unsee.
